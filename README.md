# NFHS_Orphans
This is our Data Analysis project (in 1st yr of UG) by a team of five students

The dataset records the figures on orphanhood based on background (age, wealth index etc.) and the states for the three periods in India. It provides an insight on worsening orphanhood in the lowest and second income class. Across NFHS-3, NFHS-4, and NFHS-5, one insight is that the percentage of children who have lost both parents is very small compared to those who have lost only one parent. This pattern remains almost consistent across all three rounds. It shows that single-parent loss is a more common reality than double orphanhood. Which is important when designing support policies. The definitions for orphanhood, and other variables will be same as in the NFHS reports.

Objective:
1. Whether majority of orphans have been abandoned by the father or the mother (provided both are alive)?
2. Is there a correlation and a causation b/w the percentage of the majority gender group abandoning the child and the former's divorce rates, gender income gap? (Will have to refer to a different dataset)
3. Why are there abnormally high values of orphanhood (in both parents alive and both parents dead and, the figures for 'Percentage not Living with a Biological Parent(s)') in northeastern states?
4. What is the causation behind variance of both or single parent alive (abandoning the child) across the different region-wise categories of states?
5. What are the periodic changes (2005-6, 2015-16, 2019-21) in orphanhood like increased/decreased/unchanged values, across all the categories?

By leveraging the large, statistically meaningful sample size of the NFHS, we aim to move beyond national averages to identify regional disparities and compare orphaned versus non-orphaned children across key welfare metrics.
